"既然...就..." Conditional Structure Analysis

Structure Overview

The "既然...就..." structure is a common conditional pattern in Chinese that expresses a logical relationship between a factual condition and its necessary result. Unlike hypothetical conditionals (such as "如果...就..."), this structure assumes that the condition is already established or accepted as true.

Grammatical Structure:

既然 + [Factual Condition] + 就 + [Logical Result/Inference]

  • "既然" (jìrán) - "since," "given that," introduces a fact or accepted premise
  • "就" (jiù) - "then," introduces the logical result or inference based on the premise

Sentence Analysis

Example 1:

既然Conjunction: introducing factual condition 你Subject 已经Adverbial: indicating completion 答应Verb: predicate 了Aspect particle: completion， 就Conjunction: indicating result 不要Adverbial: negation 反悔Verb: predicate。

Syntax Analysis: This sentence consists of two clauses. The first clause "既然你已经答应了" (Since you have already promised) is the condition, and the second clause "就不要反悔" (then don't go back on your word) is the result. "既然" introduces an established fact (that a promise has been made), and "就" introduces the logical conclusion based on this fact (that one shouldn't break the promise).

The reduplicated form of the verb "休息休息" indicates a short duration or light action.

"既然" introduces an established fact (that everyone agrees), and "就" introduces the decision based on this fact (to decide in this way). "这么" is a manner adverbial that refers to a way of deciding that has been mentioned before or is known to both parties.

"既然" introduces an established fact (knowing that overeating is bad), and "就" introduces the logical conclusion based on this fact (that one should change the habit). The modal adverb "应该" (should) indicates obligation or necessity.

Summary

The "既然...就..." structure is an important conditional pattern in Chinese with the following characteristics:

  • "既然" introduces a factual condition, something that already exists or is established
  • "就" introduces the result, decision, or inference based on this fact
  • This structure expresses a logical necessity or reasonableness
  • The result clause often contains modal words (like "应该" - should) or modal particles (like "吧" - indicating suggestion)

This structure is commonly used in daily communication, especially when expressing suggestions, decisions, or inferences. Mastering this structure helps improve the accuracy and naturalness of Chinese expression.
