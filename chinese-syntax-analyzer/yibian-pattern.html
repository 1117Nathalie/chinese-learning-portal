"以便" (In Order To) Pattern Analysis

← Back to Home

Pattern Overview

"以便" is a conjunction that introduces a purpose clause. It is equivalent to "in order to" or "so that" in English. This pattern is used to express the purpose or intention behind an action.

Grammatical Function:

  • Functions as a conjunction connecting two clauses
  • Introduces a purpose clause that explains why the action in the main clause is performed
  • Creates a logical relationship between cause (main clause) and purpose (subordinate clause)
  • Often follows a comma that separates the main clause from the purpose clause

Sentence Analysis

Example 1:

我Subject 早Adverbial: time 起床Verb: predicate， 以便Conjunction: purpose 赶上Verb: predicate 第一班火车Object。

Translation: I get up early in order to catch the first train.

Syntax Analysis: This sentence consists of two clauses. The first clause "我早起床" (I get up early) is the main action, and the second clause "以便赶上第一班火车" (in order to catch the first train) is the purpose clause. "以便" connects these two clauses, indicating that the first action is done for the purpose of achieving the second.

Example 2:

他Subject 记下Verb: predicate 了笔记Object， 以便Conjunction: purpose 以后Adverbial: time 复习Verb: predicate。

Translation: He took notes in order to review later.

Syntax Analysis: This sentence also consists of two clauses. The first clause "他记下了笔记" (He took notes) is the main action, and the second clause "以便以后复习" (in order to review later) is the purpose clause. "以便" introduces the purpose of the main action, and "以后" is a time adverbial that modifies "复习" (review).

Example 3:

我们Subject 提前Verb: predicate 准备Verb: predicate 材料Object， 以便Conjunction: purpose 顺利Adverbial: manner 完成Verb: predicate 项目Object。

Translation: We prepare the materials in advance in order to complete the project smoothly.

Syntax Analysis: This sentence consists of two clauses. The first clause "我们提前准备材料" (We prepare the materials in advance) is the main action, and the second clause "以便顺利完成项目" (in order to complete the project smoothly) is the purpose clause. "以便" connects these two clauses, indicating that the preparation is done for the purpose of smooth project completion. "顺利" is an adverbial of manner that modifies "完成" (complete).

Example 4:

把Preposition: disposal 自己Subject 下一周Adverbial: time 要Auxiliary verb: necessity 做Verb: predicate 的工作Object 好好Adverbial: manner 安排Verb: predicate 一下Complement: slight degree， 以便Conjunction: purpose 做到Verb: predicate 心中有数Object: idiom。

Translation: Arrange your work for next week properly in order to have a clear idea of what needs to be done.

Syntax Analysis: This sentence consists of two clauses. The first clause uses the "把" (ba) construction, where "把" is a preposition that brings the object "自己下一周要做的工作" (your work for next week) forward. "好好" (properly) is an adverbial of manner, "安排" (arrange) is the predicate verb, and "一下" is a complement indicating a slight degree. The second clause is introduced by "以便" (in order to), with "做到" (achieve) as the predicate verb and "心中有数" (have a clear idea) as an idiomatic object phrase. The subject in the second clause is omitted as it's the same as in the first clause.

The first clause uses the "把" (ba) construction, where "你" (you) is the subject, "先" (first) is a time adverbial, "把" is a preposition that brings the object "资料" (materials) forward, "准备" (prepare) is the predicate verb, and "好" is a result complement. The second clause is introduced by "以便" (in order to), with "开会时" (during the meeting) as a time adverbial and "研究" (study) as the predicate verb. The subject and object in the second clause are omitted, as they are understood from context.

In the first clause, "我" (I) is the subject, "要" (need to) is an auxiliary verb indicating necessity, "多" (more) is a degree adverbial, "看" (read) is the predicate verb, and "中文报纸和杂志" (Chinese newspapers and magazines) is the object. The second clause is introduced by "以便" (in order to), with "提高" (improve) as the predicate verb and "自己的阅读能力" (my reading ability) as the object. The subject in the second clause is omitted as it's the same as in the first clause.

Example 7:

请Verb: request 您Subject 留下Verb: predicate 宝贵意见Object， 以便Conjunction: purpose 我们Subject 能Auxiliary verb: ability 更好Adverbial: degree 地Structural particle 改进Verb: predicate 工作Object。

Translation: Please leave your valuable feedback so that we can better improve our work.

Syntax Analysis: This sentence consists of two clauses. In the first clause, "请" (please) is a verb indicating a request, "您" (you, formal) is the subject, "留下" (leave) is the predicate verb, and "宝贵意见" (valuable feedback) is the object. The second clause is introduced by "以便" (in order to), with "我们" (we) as the subject, "能" (can) as an auxiliary verb indicating ability, "更好地" (better) as an adverbial phrase (with "地" as a structural particle), "改进" (improve) as the predicate verb, and "工作" (work) as the object.

Translation: Please leave your contact information so that we can contact you.

Syntax Analysis: This sentence consists of two clauses. The first clause uses the "把" (ba) construction, where "请" (please) is a verb indicating a request, "把" is a preposition that brings the object "您的联系方式" (your contact information) forward, and "留下" (leave) is the predicate verb. The second clause is introduced by "以便" (in order to), with "我们" (we) as the subject, "能" (can) as an auxiliary verb indicating ability, "联系" (contact) as the predicate verb, and "您" (you, formal) as the object.

Usage Notes

  • Subject omission: The subject in the purpose clause is often omitted when it is the same as the subject in the main clause.
  • Similar expressions: Other conjunctions with similar meanings include "为了" (in order to), "以免" (in order not to/lest), and "好让" (so that).
  • Formal usage: "以便" is more formal than "为了" and is often used in written Chinese or formal speech.
  • Clause order: The purpose clause introduced by "以便" typically follows the main clause, though in some cases the order can be reversed for emphasis.
  • With "把" construction: "以便" often appears in sentences with the "把" construction, which is used to emphasize the disposal or handling of an object before stating the purpose.
  • In polite requests: "以便" is commonly used in polite requests or instructions, especially in formal or business contexts, as seen in examples with "请" (please).

Understanding the "以便" pattern helps learners express purpose relationships in Chinese, which is essential for constructing complex sentences and explaining motivations behind actions.
