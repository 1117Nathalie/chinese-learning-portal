Grammatical Features Summary

  • Use of Time Adverbials: In Chinese, time adverbials typically appear at the beginning of a sentence or before the subject, such as "紧张的工作之后" (after intense work) and "每到假期的时候" (whenever the holiday comes).
  • Use of Conjunctions: Conjunctions like "但是" (but) and "所以" (so) are used to connect clauses, expressing contrast, cause-effect, and other logical relationships.
  • Verb Reduplication: Forms like "休息休息" (take a rest) and "谈谈" (talk about) indicate the short duration or slight degree of an action.
  • Modal Verbs: Words like "会" (will) and "需要" (need) express possibility, necessity, and other modalities.
  • Use of Complements: Forms like "一下" (a bit) and "两年多了" (over two years) provide additional information about the time, degree, etc. of an action.
  • Position of Attributives: In Chinese, attributives typically appear before the words they modify, such as "紧张的工作" (intense work) and "自己的身心" (one's body and mind).
  • Subject Omission: In contexts where the subject is clear, Chinese often omits the subject, as in "常常会大吃大喝" (often eat and drink a lot) where the subject "我" (I) is omitted.

These sentences demonstrate various syntactic features of Chinese, including complex sentence structures, the use of adverbials, and verb reduplication forms. Understanding these syntactic features helps improve Chinese expression and comprehension abilities.
