"一连" (In Succession) Pattern Analysis

← Back to Home

Pattern Overview

"一连" is an adverbial phrase that indicates actions happening continuously or in succession. It is equivalent to "in succession" or "consecutively" in English. This pattern is used to emphasize the continuity or repetition of an action.

Grammatical Function:

  • Functions as an adverbial of manner
  • Modifies the verb to indicate continuous or consecutive actions
  • Often used with a numeral phrase to indicate the number of times or duration
  • Emphasizes the uninterrupted nature of the action

Sentence Analysis

Example 1:

他Subject 一连Adverbial: manner 打Verb: predicate 了Aspect particle: completion 三个喷嚏Object。

Translation: He sneezed three times in succession.

Syntax Analysis: In this sentence, "一连" functions as an adverbial of manner that modifies the verb "打" (sneeze). The subject is "他" (he), the predicate verb is "打" (sneeze), and the object is "三个喷嚏" (three sneezes). "一连" emphasizes that the sneezes happened one after another without interruption.

Example 2:

她Subject 一连Adverbial: manner 工作Verb: predicate 了Aspect particle: completion 三天三夜Object: duration， 没有Adverbial: negation 休息Verb: predicate。

Translation: She worked for three days and three nights in succession, without rest.

Syntax Analysis: This sentence consists of two clauses. In the first clause, "一连" functions as an adverbial of manner that modifies the verb "工作" (work). The subject is "她" (she), the predicate verb is "工作" (work), and "三天三夜" (three days and three nights) indicates the duration of the action. "一连" emphasizes the continuous nature of the work without interruption, which is further reinforced by the second clause "没有休息" (without rest).

Syntax Analysis: This sentence consists of two clauses. In the first clause, "一连" functions as an adverbial of manner that modifies the verb "提" (ask). The subject is "老师" (teacher), the predicate verb is "提" (ask), and the object is "五个问题" (five questions). "一连" emphasizes that the questions were asked one after another without interruption. The second clause describes the result of this continuous questioning.

Usage Notes

  • Numerical expression: "一连" is typically followed by a verb and then a numerical expression indicating the number of times or duration of the action.
  • Emphasis on continuity: The primary function of "一连" is to emphasize that actions happen continuously without interruption.
  • Similar expressions: Other expressions with similar meanings include "接连" (in succession), "连续" (continuously), and "不断" (incessantly).
  • Common contexts: "一连" is often used to describe repeated actions (sneezing, coughing), extended periods of activity (working, studying), or sequential events (asking questions, making attempts).

Understanding the "一连" pattern helps learners express continuous or consecutive actions in Chinese, which is useful for describing sequences of events or emphasizing the persistence of an action.
